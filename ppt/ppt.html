Automatic Shift Assignment

swap_calls

Proximity Logic Overview

The system independently determines the correct shift by analyzing actual punch times, intentionally discarding flawed CLM-assigned data.

Priority 1: Rotational

A / B / C Shifts

Match evaluated within ±2 hours of shift start time. Used for primary rotational shifts to ensure tight alignment with standard scheduling windows.

info Includes sophisticated Cross-Midnight Handling for Night Shift (C).

Priority 2: General

G / W1 Shifts

Evaluated via an average-difference score algorithm. Statistical evaluation for non-rotational shifts, calculating the least variance across the entire shift duration.

Duty-In Reconciliation Logic

login
Scenario Condition Assigned Duty-In
Early OT Arrived > 59 min early AND inOtAllowed = true Rounded UP to next 30-minute mark
Grace Arrived within 15 min late OR slightly early Exact Shift Start Time
Penalty Arrived > 15 min late Rounded UP to next 30-minute mark
Example Calculation: Shift 08:30 | Arrived 08:47 (17 min late) → Duty In: 09:00

Duty-Out Reconciliation Logic

logout
Scenario Condition Assigned Duty-Out
Penalty Left before shift end time Rounded DOWN to previous 30-minute mark
OT Out Stayed past shift end AND outOtAllowed = true Rounded DOWN to previous 30-minute mark
Normal Left at or after shift end, no OT permissions Capped at Exact Shift End Time
Example Calculation: Shift ends 17:30 | Left 17:15 (Early departure) → Duty Out: 17:00

Overtime & Net Calculation

$$ \text{Total OT} = \lfloor \text{In-OT} + \text{Out-OT} \rfloor_{0.5h} $$

* All OT is floored to strict 0.5-hour increments to ensure compliance with billing standards.

$$ \text{Total Working Hours} = \text{Duty Hours} + \text{Total OT} $$
